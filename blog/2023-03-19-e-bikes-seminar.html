E-Bikes: The New Frontier — Electric Bike Safety Seminar

March 19, 2023  |  Chuck Glover

Electric bicycles are transforming the way people commute, recreate, and perform their jobs. What was once a niche product has become a mainstream mode of transportation — and with that growth comes a pressing need for proper training and understanding of e-bike operation.

Why E-Bikes Require Special Training

Unlike conventional bicycles, e-bikes have electrical assistance that amplifies the rider's pedaling input. This creates several unique handling characteristics:

  • Acceleration feel: The motor assists immediately, giving a surge that novice riders may not expect, especially when starting from a stop.
  • Higher speeds: E-bikes can sustain speeds of 28 km/h or more on flat ground, bringing riders into faster traffic environments than they may be accustomed to.
  • Braking distances: Higher speeds mean longer braking distances. Riders must anticipate stops earlier and apply brakes progressively.
  • Weight: E-bikes are heavier than standard bicycles due to the motor and battery, affecting handling, especially at low speeds and when lifting the bike.

Legal Requirements in British Columbia

In BC, e-bikes are classified as power-assisted bicycles and must meet specific criteria:

  • Maximum motor output of 500W
  • Maximum speed of 32 km/h on level ground with motor assistance
  • Must have functional pedals
  • Rider must be 16 years or older
  • Helmet is mandatory for all e-bike riders
